As the sample moves through the gel, components are sepa-
rated into bands down the length of the gel. As mentioned, 
larger molecules will move more slowly through the gel. If the 
sample has received a dye treatment, the results will appear as distinct bands (Figure 5). These bands can 
be correlated to a molecular marker (or standard) to determine the molecular weight of the sample in each 
band. This provides evidence which can lead to the biomolecular identification of each component. Electro-
phoresis can be used to compare genomes of different organisms or individuals, as well as to locate and 
identify a specific gene of interest. 

Recombinant DNA 

DNA can be modified and re-inserted into the original source, or into another organism, if the nucleotide se-
quence is available. DNA molecules that are transferred from one organism into another are referred to as 
recombinant DNA. This technique is very useful and frequently applied in research or when creating hybrid 
species. For example, many fruit or vegetable crops integrate recombinant DNA to add herbicide resistance 
or drought tolerance.
