Transformation 

Vectors take shape as a variety of things, but plasmids are most commonly used. Plasmids are small, circu-
lar, extra-chromosomal, pieces of DNA (2000 - 10,000 base pairs) that are naturally found in bacteria. They 
became useful molecular biology tools when it was discovered that a foreign gene could be inserted into the 
plasmid and then transferred into a bacterial cell. This process is called transformation. After the plasmid 
gets transferred into a bacterial cell (with the foreign DNA sequence) the transformed bacteria can be ex-
pected to produce the new gene product(s). The process of transformation is delineated in Figure 6. This 
technique has enabled huge advances in the fields of molecular biology and genomics.
